What Are Alfajores? A Guide to Peruvian Pastries

Peruvian pastries are one of the most quietly special things on our menu, and the ones guests remember most. They are not common in Northern Virginia, which is exactly why a tray of them turns a dessert table into a conversation. Here is a short guide to the classics, and how to bring them to your own gathering.

Alfajores

The star of the table. Alfajores are delicate, melt-in-your-mouth shortbread cookies sandwiched with dulce de leche and dusted with powdered sugar. Soft, buttery, not too sweet, they are the pastry people reach for again and again.

Milhojas

Layers of crisp pastry stacked with cream and dulce de leche, finished with sugar. The name means "a thousand sheets," and the texture is exactly that, shatter-light and rich at once.

Pionono

A tender rolled sponge with a sweet filling, sliced into elegant spirals. It is light, pretty on a platter, and a lovely contrast to the denser pastries.

Empanadas

Golden hand pies with savory or sweet fillings. They round out a spread beautifully and are a favorite for events that run from afternoon into evening.

Tres leches

The classic "three milks" cake, soaked until impossibly moist and topped with cream. A crowd favorite that works as a dessert centerpiece or alongside the rest of the table.

How to order

Everything is made from scratch with authentic recipes, and pastries are available by the dozen. You can mix an assortment for an event, build a dessert table, or send a box as a gift. Give as much notice as you can, especially for events and large orders, and we will confirm what works for your date. See the full selection on the Peruvian pastries page.
